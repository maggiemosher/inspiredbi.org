Best Practices

In-Depth Tips for Safe & Ethical AI Integration

Practical guardrails for every area of practice — ensuring AI augments educator expertise rather than replacing it.

Curriculum Development: Human-in-the-Loop Mandate

AI generates the first draft, never the final product. Every unit plan, lesson, and scaffold produced by an AI tool must pass through educator review before it reaches students. Additionally, ground all AI-assisted curriculum in evidence-based frameworks — Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and CASEL SEL competencies — to ensure both rigor and inclusivity.

Teaching Excellence: Transparent Research and Citations

When using AI to synthesize educational research for PLCs or coaching sessions, always verify primary sources independently and cite originals — not the AI summary. Additionally, de-identify all classroom data before inputting it into any AI platform. Remove student names, IDs, and any personally identifiable information before asking an AI to analyze assessment trends or student work samples.

Assessment & Evaluation: Formative Over Summative

Prioritize AI in low-stakes, formative contexts where its role is to support learning rather than render final judgments on student achievement. AI-generated grades or evaluations should always be reviewed and finalized by a human educator. Additionally, when using AI to generate rubrics, share those rubrics with students before the task begins — rubric transparency is fundamental to fair assessment practice.
