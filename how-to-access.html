How to Access Free AI Step by Step

You already have access to powerful free AI tools. This guide shows you exactly where to go and what to click — in 20 minutes or less.

Jump to: Gemini Pro NotebookLM Workspace AI Consumer vs. Educational
Google Gemini

Gemini Pro — Step-by-Step Access

Gemini is Google's flagship AI — and educators have multiple free pathways to access it. This guide walks you through each level from basic access to deep research and educational integrations.

🔵 Consumer Gemini vs. Educational Gemini

There are two different versions of Gemini you might encounter. Consumer Gemini (free at gemini.google.com with a personal Gmail account) is powerful but your conversations may be used to improve Google's models. Educational Gemini (accessed through your school or university Google Workspace account) is FERPA compliant, never uses your data to train AI, and has no advertising. If you are working with any student information, always use your educational account.

1

Go to Gemini and Sign In

Go to gemini.google.com and sign in with your Google account. Use your school or university Google account for educational protections. Use your personal Gmail only for non-student content.

Open Gemini →
2

Find Gemini in Your Google Workspace Apps

If your institution has Google Workspace for Education, Gemini is integrated directly into Gmail, Docs, Slides, and Sheets. Look for the sparkle icon (✨) or a "Help me write" button in the sidebar of any Google app. This is the educational, FERPA-compliant version.

💡 If you don't see the Gemini button, your institution may not have a paid tier. Ask your IT admin which Google Workspace tier you have.
3

Check Your Institution's Tier for Advanced Features

To access advanced features like Deep Research, Imagen (image generation), and Gemini Gems (custom agents), check if your school has the Teaching & Learning Add-on or Google AI Pro for Education tier. Ask your IT administrator or check your Google Admin console.

💡 See the Google Workspace Tier Comparison on the Safe & Secure page for a full feature breakdown by tier.
4

Start a Conversation in Standalone Gemini

For the standalone Gemini experience, visit gemini.google.com and start chatting — it's free with any Google account. Use it for lesson planning, email drafting, research summaries, and more.

5

Enable Deep Research

Click the research icon in Gemini (it looks like a magnifying glass with a sparkle) to activate Deep Research mode. This sends Gemini on a multi-source research task — it browses the web, synthesizes findings, and returns a comprehensive report with citations. Excellent for literature reviews and grant research.

💡 Deep Research is available in Google AI Pro for Education and in personal Gemini Advanced subscriptions.
6

Try NotebookLM at notebooklm.google.com

Upload your curriculum documents and ask questions directly from your own materials. NotebookLM can generate quizzes, study guides, and a podcast-style audio summary of anything you upload — completely free.

Open NotebookLM (Free) →
7

Explore Workspace AI Features

In Google Docs, click "Help me write" to draft content from a prompt. In Slides, click the Gemini icon to generate a presentation from your notes. In Gmail, Gemini can draft, reply, and summarize long email threads.

💡 These features are available in Google Workspace Education Plus and higher tiers — not in the free Education Fundamentals tier.
Free AI Research Tool

NotebookLM — The Educator's Secret Weapon

NotebookLM is Google's free AI research assistant that answers questions exclusively from documents you upload. It doesn't hallucinate beyond your sources — making it the safest and most curriculum-aligned AI tool available.

1

Open NotebookLM

Go to notebooklm.google.com and sign in with your Google account. It's completely free — no payment required.

2

Create a New Notebook

Click New Notebook and give it a name (e.g., "Unit 3 — Civil Rights Movement" or "PRISM Grant Materials").

3

Upload Your Sources

Click Add Sources to upload PDFs, paste text, or link Google Docs. You can add up to 50 sources per notebook (up to 500 on the integrated education tier). NotebookLM will only answer from these sources.

4

Ask Questions About Your Materials

Type any question in the chat — "What does this rubric require for an A?" or "Summarize the key themes across these readings." NotebookLM cites the exact passage it's drawing from, so you can verify every answer.

5

Generate a Study Guide or Quiz

Click Notebook Guide in the left sidebar to auto-generate a study guide, FAQ, timeline, or practice quiz from your uploaded materials. Perfect for student review packets.

6

Create an Audio Overview

Click Audio Overview to generate a 10-minute podcast-style conversation between two AI hosts discussing your uploaded content. Share it with students as a review tool — it's engaging and accessible for auditory learners.

💡 Students who struggle with reading find Audio Overview transformative for review and comprehension.

Best Educator Uses for NotebookLM

  • Upload your syllabus and rubrics — let students query it directly (create a shareable notebook)
  • Upload grant RFPs and your draft narrative — ask NotebookLM to identify alignment gaps
  • Upload 10 research articles and ask for a synthesized literature review outline
  • Upload IEP goals and ask for plain-language translations and home activity ideas
  • Use Audio Overview to create an instant podcast review episode for any unit
Already in Your Google Apps

Google Workspace AI — Features You Already Have

If your institution uses Google Workspace for Education (Education Plus or higher), you already have AI built into the tools you use every day. Here's where to find it.

📝

Google Docs

Click "Help me write" in the sidebar to draft from a prompt. Highlight any text and click the Gemini icon to rewrite, summarize, or expand. Use "Help me improve" to polish writing without rewriting it entirely.

📧

Gmail

Use "Help me write" to draft emails from a short description. Click "Summarize this email" on long threads. Gemini can draft replies in your tone based on the conversation history.

📊

Google Slides

Click the Gemini icon to generate an entire presentation from notes or a topic description. Gemini creates slides, layouts, and speaker notes. Use it to turn lesson plans into presentations in minutes.

🗓

Google Meet

AI-generated meeting notes and transcripts are available in Education Plus and higher. After the meeting, Gemini sends a summary of key points and action items directly to your Google Doc.

🎓

Google Classroom

Practice Sets (Education Plus+) use AI to provide step-by-step hints to students who are stuck, without giving away the answer. The teacher sees which students used hints and at which steps.

✨

AI Rubric Assistant

Available in the Teaching & Learning Add-on and Google AI Pro tiers. Generate rubrics from assignment descriptions, or upload an existing rubric and ask Gemini to refine the performance descriptors.

Critical Distinction

Consumer AI vs. Educational AI — Know the Difference

The most important thing any educator needs to understand before using AI: consumer versions and educational versions of the same tool have very different privacy protections.

⚠️ Consumer AI (Use with Caution)

  • ✗Conversations may train AI models
  • ✗Not FERPA compliant
  • ✗No institutional admin controls
  • ✗May show ads or recommendations

Examples: ChatGPT free, Gemini (personal Gmail), Claude free tier

✓ Educational AI (Use for Student Work)

  • ✓Never trains models on your data
  • ✓FERPA and COPPA compliant
  • ✓Admin console with full controls
  • ✓No advertising to students

Examples: Google Workspace Education, Microsoft 365 Education, SchoolAI, Khanmigo, Mizou

Simple Rule for Educators

If you are working with any student information — names, grades, IEP goals, behavioral data, health information — use only your institutionally approved educational AI tools. For personal productivity (your own research, lesson planning without student names, professional writing), consumer tools are generally acceptable. When in doubt, ask your district or university IT department.
