A Note on Privacy and "Agentic" Behavior

Privacy Disclaimer "Compliance" depends on how your district sets up the integration. Always confirm a Data Privacy Agreement (DPA) is on file before rostering students.
Agentic vs. Chatbot Agentic tools follow multi-step instructions (rubric → hint workflows). Basic chatbots respond prompt-by-prompt without managing the session.
Data Usage Assume data may be used for model training unless the vendor states otherwise. For student work, prefer enterprise/district privacy settings.

CIDDL · Center for Innovation, Design, and Digital Learning

🧠 CIDDL AI Studio

A collaborative, 6-week national learning community for educators, faculty, and leaders to critically explore the social, ethical, and human dimensions of AI in education — with a strong focus on accessibility and students with disabilities.

🧠 What is CIDDL AI Studio?

Rather than basic "how-to" tool training, CIDDL AI Studio focuses on human-centered AI, human variability, and designing responsible AI-supported learning environments. Participants engage in facilitated community discussions, explore real-world equity implications, and leave with frameworks for integrating AI accessibly and ethically into their practice.

ciddl.org/ciddl-artificial-intelligence/ →

1

Visit the CIDDL AI Page

Go to ciddl.org/ciddl-artificial-intelligence/ to learn about current and upcoming AI Studio cohorts, including registration dates and participation requirements.

2

Register for a Cohort

AI Studio runs in facilitated cohorts over 6 weeks. Registration is free. Sign up for the next available session to join educators and leaders from across the country exploring responsible AI together.

3

Engage with the Community

Each week focuses on a different dimension of AI in education — from ethics and equity to accessibility and human variability. Participate in facilitated discussions, collaborative activities, and peer reflection that go well beyond tool tutorials.

4

Apply Frameworks to Your Context

Leave each session with concrete frameworks and strategies for designing AI-supported learning environments that are accessible, ethical, and centered on the full diversity of learners — including students with disabilities.

✨ Why CIDDL Stands Out

  • One of the only free AI professional learning programs specifically designed around disability, accessibility, and human variability
  • Grounded in research — CIDDL is a federally funded center with deep ties to special education and universal design
  • Cohort-based format builds real community with educators facing similar challenges
  • Complements tool-focused training by addressing the ethical and human dimensions most courses skip
  • Certificates of completion available for participating educators
VOISS · Project iKNOW · University of Kansas

🥽 AI & Immersive Social Skills Training — VOISS

Backed by extensive research from the University of Kansas, VOISS uses virtual reality and AI to help students — especially those with autism and learning disabilities — build real-world social-emotional skills in a safe, simulated environment.

🥽 What is VOISS?

VOISS (Virtual Reality and AI for Social Skills) is a research-backed platform that places students inside immersive, realistic social scenarios — a job interview, a classroom disagreement, a first day at a new school — and uses AI to guide them through evidence-based social skill practice. The companion VOISS Advisor dashboard gives educators progress-monitoring tools, AI-generated feedback, and learning paths to help students generalize skills from the virtual environment into their daily lives.

voiss.org/voissadvisor →

1

Access VOISS Advisor

Go to voiss.org/voissadvisor to access free educator resources, evidence-based video clips, and information about the VOISS platform and Project iKNOW research.

2

Explore the Free Video Library

VOISS Advisor includes a free library of video-based social skills scenarios that can be used in any classroom — no VR hardware required. Browse scenarios by social skill target, age group, and setting.

3

Set Up Student Learning Paths

Use the VOISS Advisor dashboard to assign specific social skill learning paths to individual students. The AI tracks progress, identifies patterns, and suggests next steps based on each student's performance across scenarios.

4

Connect to Project iKNOW

Project iKNOW is the research arm of VOISS — connecting educators to the latest evidence-based findings on AI and XR for social skill development. Use the research library to ground your practice in peer-reviewed evidence and stay current on what the science says about immersive learning for students with disabilities.

✨ Why VOISS is Unique

  • Developed and rigorously researched at the University of Kansas — one of the leading institutions in special education and learning science
  • Free video resources are usable in any classroom without VR hardware or special equipment
  • AI-powered progress monitoring gives educators data-driven insight into social skill development over time
  • Addresses a critical gap — most AI tools ignore students with autism and social-emotional learning needs entirely
  • Designed for generalization — skills practiced in the virtual environment are explicitly connected to real-world application

Go to the course page on Coursera and click "Enroll for Free." To take the course completely free, select the Audit option during checkout — this grants full access to all video lectures and reading materials. The paid track is only required if you want the official verified certificate.

Top modules to start with:

1

What is AI?

A jargon-free breakdown of common terminology, clarifying the often-confused differences between machine learning, deep learning, and data science — essential context for any educator teaching or discussing AI.

2

Building AI Projects

Provides a realistic framework for what AI is actually capable of, helping you spot opportunities to apply AI to problems in your own organization. Especially useful for instructional coaches and administrators.

3

AI and Society

A vital module covering how AI systems can inherit bias, how to diminish those biases, and how AI will impact jobs and the broader economy — directly applicable to classroom discussions about responsible AI use.

✨ Coursera AI for Everyone Tips

  • The course is non-technical — no math or coding background required whatsoever
  • Designed to be completed in about 6 hours — ideal for a weekend or a week of 30-minute sessions
  • The AI and Society module pairs perfectly with classroom debates on AI ethics and bias
  • Andrew Ng's clear explanations make this ideal for sharing with parents or school board members seeking an AI overview

Deeper Practice

Curriculum, Teaching & Assessment Strategies

Practical ideas for applying AI across the core work of education — from building rigorous curriculum to evaluating authentic student learning.

🏫 Curriculum Development

Building rigorous, standards-aligned curriculum that integrates 21st-century skills and AI-enriched learning experiences.

  • Unit and lesson design Utilizing AI as a collaborative thought partner to brainstorm phenomena-based learning sequences, generate creative hooks, and build robust unit outlines in a fraction of the time.
  • Standards alignment mapping Leveraging AI tools to cross-reference lesson materials against state and national standards, instantly identifying instructional gaps and ensuring comprehensive coverage.
  • Cross-disciplinary frameworks Using large language models to uncover natural intersections between subjects — such as integrating historical primary sources into a statistics lesson — to create cohesive, project-based learning.
  • Differentiated learning pathways Rapidly generating multiple reading levels of the same core text, translating materials, and creating customized scaffolds to ensure rigorous content remains accessible to every learner.

📝 Teaching Excellence

Empowering educators through coaching, mentorship, and professional learning communities that elevate classroom practice in an AI-driven era.

  • Instructional coaching cycles Using AI-assisted analysis of lesson plans and teacher reflections to help coaches pinpoint specific, high-leverage goals for individual educators.
  • Peer learning communities Empowering PLCs to use AI to quickly synthesize educational research, analyze grade-level trends, and co-create shared instructional resources.
  • Classroom observation & feedback Incorporating AI-powered audio analysis tools to objectively measure teacher talk-time versus student talk-time, providing immediate, non-evaluative data on classroom engagement.
  • Teacher leadership development Cultivating "AI Champions" within buildings to lead pilot programs, evaluate new educational technologies, and guide peers in ethical, effective AI implementation.

📊 Assessment & Evaluation

Developing meaningful assessment strategies that measure authentic learning and utilize AI to inform instruction at every stage.

  • Formative assessment design Instantly generating diverse, low-stakes retrieval practice — exit tickets, targeted multiple-choice questions, and short-answer prompts — to check for understanding daily.
  • Performance-based tasks Prompting AI to design complex, real-world scenarios and detailed, multi-tiered rubrics that evaluate critical thinking rather than simple memorization.
  • Data analysis & reporting Securely using data-interpreter tools to spot learning gaps across qualitative student responses, turning overwhelming spreadsheets of assessment data into actionable instructional steps.
  • Portfolio and mastery models Utilizing AI platforms to help teachers efficiently map student artifacts and project outcomes to specific mastery standards over the course of a semester.
