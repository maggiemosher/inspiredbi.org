Individualized Learning & Teacher Productivity

1 MagicSchool AI magicschool.ai Teacher Productivity & Student Bots The ultimate teacher toolkit with 60+ free tools for lesson plans, rubrics, assessments, communication, and more. Most bots free with some usage limits.
2 Gemini for Education gemini.google.com Brainstorming & General Assistant An excellent all-purpose AI from Google for brainstorming ideas, explaining concepts, and acting as a creative partner. Experimental integrations in Docs, etc., often accessible via Google Labs.
3 NotebookLM notebooklm.google.com Research & Document Analysis Lets you "chat" with your own documents, create extension questions, summaries, podcasts, mind maps and more. Free with a Google account. Upload PDFs, text, URLs — get summaries, mind maps, and podcasts.
4 Suno suno.ai Music & Audio Creation The leading tool for turning ideas or text into full songs with vocals and instruments, making content creation highly engaging. Free credits that renew daily (~50 credits/10 songs) for non-commercial use.
5 Goblin Tools goblin.tools Student Support & Organization A simple, brilliant set of tools designed to support executive functioning for all students, especially neurodiverse learners. The entire suite (Magic ToDo, Formalizer, Judge, etc.) is completely free.
6 Napkin AI napkin.ai Idea Visualization & Connection Offers a unique, non-linear way to connect ideas and visualize thoughts, moving beyond traditional note-taking. Free plan to create and connect a limited number of notes ("napkins").
7 Brisk Teaching briskteaching.com Differentiation & Feedback Integrates seamlessly into a teacher's workflow (Google Docs, Chrome) to provide immediate, practical support. Free version allows text leveling, feedback, and resource generation with daily usage limits.
8 Eduaide.AI eduaide.ai Comprehensive Educator AI Suite Generate lesson plans, assessments, rubrics, and differentiated materials from a single prompt — one of the most versatile all-in-one educator platforms available. Free tier available with a generous set of generation tools covering the full range of educator workflows.
9 TeachAid teachaid.ai Adaptive Learning Support Personalizes learning pathways and provides adaptive instructional support based on individual student needs, performance data, and learning goals. Free tier available for educators to explore adaptive support tools and personalized learning features.
10 Mizou mizou.com Student Chatbots & Formative Assessment Build private AI chatbots students interact with via a simple shared link — no student accounts or PII required. Supports automated grading, rubric-aligned feedback, and 50+ languages. Free tier includes chatbot creation and deployment; students access via link with no signup required.
11 PlayLab playlab.ai Custom AI Tool Builder A nonprofit platform that lets educators build custom AI applications aligned to specific pedagogical goals — no coding required. Ideal for creating reusable, shareable classroom AI tools. Completely free with a nonprofit mission — build, publish, and share custom AI tools with your school community.
12 Goblin Tools goblin.tools Student Support & Executive Function A brilliant set of micro-tools designed to support executive functioning for all students, especially neurodiverse learners — breaks tasks down, adjusts tone, and estimates effort. The entire suite (Magic ToDo, Formalizer, Judge, Estimator, etc.) is completely free with no account required.

About the Founder

Dr. Maggie Mosher

Dr. Maggie Mosher is a pioneer at the intersection of artificial intelligence (AI), extended reality (XR), student goal attainment, and educational innovation. Currently serving as the Chief AI Consultant for the Center for Reimagining Education and an assistant research professor at the University of Kansas, she leads national efforts to integrate AI into K–12 and higher education systems. As an adjunct professor at Johns Hopkins University, she develops and instructs advanced graduate coursework on strategic design, Unity, C#, and AI integration for enterprise XR applications.

Armed with a Ph.D. from the nation's #1-ranked special education program, Dr. Mosher bridges the gap between high-level technological innovation and practical learning realities. As a co-principal investigator managing over $15 million in grant funding, she has spearheaded groundbreaking XR assessment and instructional methods. Her flagship program, VOISS, utilizes immersive XR scenarios to deliver real-time, individualized instruction across over 180 social skills across 142 VR scenarios, scaling accessible, evidence-based interventions for neurodiverse learners globally.

Dr. Mosher's expertise is grounded in over two and a half decades of front-line educational leadership, ranging from an award-winning special education teacher to an assistant principal and district technology trainer. Because she has trained educators across 60 domestic school districts and 12 international municipalities, she possesses a rare, deep-rooted understanding of the systemic barriers to technology adoption. Today, she uses that insight to co-create AI professional credentialing pathways, advising schools, universities, and businesses on how to move beyond theoretical AI to scale ethical, universally designed, and personalized instructional frameworks.

A highly sought-after global thought leader, Dr. Mosher has authored numerous peer-reviewed publications and delivered over 165 presentations, including over 65 invited keynotes. Her transformative work has been honored by the American Academy of Achievement, L'Oréal Women in Science, and the International Science and Engineering Society. Recognized as a William E. Simon Fellow and a State of Kansas Horizon Award winner, she has collaborated on sustainable technology and educational panels with prominent figures including visionary filmmaker George Lucas, Nobel Laureate Baruch Blumberg, and presidential candidate Dr. Ben Carson.

Whether organizing international outreach missions for hundreds of university students or designing the future of spatial computing, Dr. Mosher remains fiercely dedicated to a single mission: developing ethical, evidence-based AI and XR strategies that build communities of respect, collaboration, and opportunity for all learners.

AI Advocates

Drs. Maggie Mosher and Lisa Dieker are faculty at the University of Kansas, home to the #1 ranked special education program in the nation, where they are known for turning big ideas into real classroom impact. Co-authors of Empowered by AI and co-hosts of AI Advocates — a podcast of quick 5 to 8-minute episodes that make free, practical AI simple for educators — they make AI fun.

Dr. Mosher is a leader at the intersection of AI and extended reality with more than $15 million in funded work and 25 years of K–12 experience, including creating immersive tools that support learners with disabilities. Dr. Dieker is the Williamson Family Distinguished Professor and director of the FLITE Center, a globally recognized leader who has secured over $30 million in funding, published extensively, and even holds six patents. Together, they combine lived classroom experience and cutting-edge research to help educators save time, expand impact, and reimagine what is possible with AI.
