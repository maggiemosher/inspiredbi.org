Setup Guide

SchoolAI — Step-by-Step Setup

SchoolAI is the most teacher-friendly option for K–12 classrooms. Students access via a link — no login required. You see every conversation in a clean dashboard.

1

Create Your Teacher Account

Go to schoolai.com and sign up with your school email. The free tier is fully functional for monitoring.

2

Create a Space

A "Space" is a monitored AI environment you design for students. Click New Space, give it a name, and write a system prompt defining the AI's role — e.g., "You are a reading coach for 6th grade students. Never give away direct answers. Ask one guiding question at a time."

3

Set Guardrails

Add topics the AI should avoid, keywords to flag (these trigger a teacher alert), and configure the SafeChat filter. SchoolAI blocks inappropriate content by default and flags student distress signals.

4

Share the Student Link

Copy the student link from your Space. Students click it and start chatting — no account creation, no personal data entry required. You can also set a student name entry prompt so conversations are labeled.

5

Review the Dashboard

Access your teacher dashboard to view all student conversations in real-time or after class. View session lengths, total messages, and any flagged keywords. Use the logs as formative assessment data — they reveal student misconceptions better than many quizzes.

Setup Guide

MagicSchool (MagicStudent) — Step-by-Step Setup

MagicSchool's MagicStudent feature gives students a supervised AI workspace while giving teachers a clear log of every interaction. Excellent for building AI literacy in a structured environment.

1

Create Your Free Teacher Account

Go to magicschool.ai and sign up. MagicStudent is the student-facing version with teacher monitoring built in.

2

Create a Class Roster

Add students by uploading a roster CSV or generate a class code. Students join via the code — no email required for younger students. Fully FERPA and COPPA compliant.

3

Select Allowed Tools

Choose which AI tools students can access from MagicSchool's library. Restrict to only the tools aligned with your lesson goals — prevent open-ended browsing of all features.

4

Assign an Activity

Create a Custom Chatbot activity or select from MagicSchool's library of pre-built tools. Students interact with the AI around your specific prompt or curriculum materials.

5

Review Usage Logs

Access the student activity dashboard to see prompts submitted, frequency of use, responses received, and time on task. Look for students who send only one prompt — a signal they're passively accepting output rather than engaging critically.

BoodleBox — Step-by-Step Setup

BoodleBox is the most flexible option — allowing you to choose your underlying AI model and build knowledge-base-grounded agents. Ideal for secondary and higher education AI literacy projects.

1

Create Your Educator Account

Go to box.boodle.ai and create a free educator account. The platform is COPPA compliant for K–12 use.

2

Create a Boodle

A "Boodle" is a custom AI environment. Set a detailed system prompt defining the agent's role, choose a base model (Claude, GPT-4, etc.), and optionally upload documents for a curriculum-specific knowledge base.

3

Add Students or Generate a Link

Invite students by email or generate a shareable link. All conversations are automatically logged and attributed to the student's session under your teacher account.

4

Monitor the Archive

Review the full conversation archive from your dashboard. Each session is timestamped, shows the student's prompts and the AI's responses, and can be exported for grading or portfolio assessment.

Mizou — Step-by-Step Setup

Mizou is an AI platform designed for creating safe, privacy-friendly educational chatbots. Students can interact with your custom bots via text or voice without needing an account, and the platform explicitly blocks data from being used to train AI models.

What is Mizou?

Mizou lets educators build custom AI chatbots grounded in their own materials and learning objectives. Students access bots via a simple link — no account, no personal information required. The teacher dashboard provides full conversation logs, voice interaction replays, automated grading, and performance analytics. FERPA and COPPA compliant.

mizou.com →

1

Create a Teacher Account

Go to mizou.com and create a free teacher account. The platform is FERPA and COPPA compliant, guaranteeing student privacy.

2

Design Your Chatbot

Choose a pre-made chatbot or build your own from scratch. Provide a detailed system prompt defining the bot's persona and specific learning objectives, and upload any relevant documents or worksheets for it to reference.

3

Deploy and Share

Review the Dashboard

Access your teacher dashboard to review detailed insights from every session. You can track performance metrics, grade interactions, and read the text logs (or listen to voice interactions) to assess student understanding.

Setup Guide

Brisk Teaching (Brisk Boost) — Step-by-Step Setup

Brisk Boost is a feature within the Brisk Teaching Chrome extension that allows teachers to turn any article, Google Doc, or webpage into an interactive, monitored AI activity for students in seconds.

What is Brisk Boost?

Brisk Teaching is a free Chrome extension with over 40 built-in AI tools for educators. Its Boost feature turns any existing online resource — a webpage, Google Doc, PDF, or YouTube video — into a structured, monitored AI activity. Students engage with the AI around your specific content while you track their progress in real time through the Brisk dashboard.

briskteaching.com →

1

Install the Extension

Go to briskteaching.com, add the free Brisk Chrome extension, and pin it to your browser toolbar. Sign up with your school Google or Microsoft account.

2

Find Your Material

Open the Google Doc, PDF, webpage, or YouTube video you want to use for the lesson. Click the Brisk "B" icon in the bottom right corner of your screen.

3

Boost the Activity

Select Boost Student Activity from the Brisk menu. Set the learning objectives, choose the interactive activity type, and define the role the AI should play (e.g., debate partner, tutor, or feedback provider).

4

Share with Students

Generate the activity link and share it with your class. Students click the link to enter a safe, structured space where they engage with the AI around your specific content.

5

Monitor Real-Time Engagement

As students work, track their progress through your Brisk activities dashboard. You can view their engagement in real-time, see their interactions with the AI, and identify where they need additional support.

Socrat — Step-by-Step Setup

Socrat focuses heavily on the Socratic method, allowing teachers to create custom AI tutors that guide students through inquiry and questioning rather than just dispensing direct answers.

What is Socrat?

Socrat is a free platform that lets teachers build custom AI bots designed around Socratic dialogue — bots that guide students with questions rather than handing over answers. Teachers can monitor live chat logs, set up class rosters with join codes, and review conversation threads to identify misconceptions and drop-off points.

socrat.ai →

1

Create an Account

Sign up for a free teacher account on socrat.ai to access the class and bot creation tools.

2

Set Up Your Class

Create a class roster or generate a join code. This allows you to organize your students and attribute their conversation logs correctly to specific individuals.

3

Build a Socratic Bot

Design a custom bot by setting its prompt. For example, instruct it to act as a "Semi-Socratic Tutor" that primarily guides students with questions but occasionally provides crucial direct information to keep them from getting hopelessly stuck.

4

Assign and Monitor

Share the bot with your students. Use the teacher dashboard to monitor live chat logs. Reviewing these threads helps you identify common misconceptions and see exactly where students drop off.

Edcafe AI — Step-by-Step Setup

Edcafe AI allows teachers to build highly customized AI tutors grounded in their own uploaded materials, featuring robust per-student analytics and specific alerts for off-task behavior.

What is Edcafe AI?

Edcafe AI is an all-in-one platform for creating curriculum-grounded AI tutors with a strong emphasis on per-student monitoring. Upload your own materials, build a custom bot with a detailed persona, share via link or QR code, and review full chat threads — including AI-generated summaries and off-task behavior alerts — from a central analytics dashboard.

edcafe.ai →

1

Create a Free Account

Go to edcafe.ai and sign up for a free educator account. The platform organizes your creations by class or unit in a central library.

2

Build Your AI Tutor

Select the chatbot builder and upload your specific course materials, rubrics, or PDFs. Use the custom instructions field to define the AI's exact persona — such as telling it to "always use pizza analogies for fractions" or to act as a specific historical figure.

3

Share via Link or QR Code

Generate a shareable link or a QR code for your newly created chatbot. Students access the AI seamlessly without needing complex setups.

4

Review Chat Threads and Alerts

Monitor the analytics dashboard, which provides full chat threads per student. Use the built-in filters to spot unread conversations, view AI-generated summaries of student progress, and check for alerts regarding off-task behavior.

5

Refine the Instructions

Based on the real conversations you review, go back and tweak the chatbot's instructions. If you notice it struggles to explain word problems or its tone is too dry, adjust the prompt to improve the tutor for the next session.

What to Actually Look For

Pedagogical Guide: What Teachers Should Monitor

Monitoring AI use isn't about catching cheating — it's about understanding how students think and building the metacognitive skills they need to use AI responsibly and effectively throughout their careers.

Prompt Iteration

Does the student refine their prompt after receiving an unsatisfactory response? This is the most reliable indicator of AI literacy. A student who sends one prompt and accepts the response passively is at cognitive risk. A student who argues with the AI, challenges its output, or refines their question is developing critical thinking skills.

Look for: Multiple conversational turns, directional language ("that's not right, try again with..."), and self-correction after reviewing AI output.

Fact-Checking & Verification

Do students treat AI output as authoritative, or do they attempt to verify claims? Monitor whether students ask the AI for sources, cross-reference with uploaded curriculum materials, or flag responses as uncertain or potentially wrong.

Instructional move: Build a "Verify Before You Trust" step into all AI-assisted assignments. Require students to cite one verification source for every factual AI claim they include in their final work.

Cognitive Offloading vs. Cognitive Scaffolding

Cognitive offloading = using AI to bypass thinking entirely (asking it to write the essay, solve the problem, or complete the project).

Cognitive scaffolding = using AI to support thinking (asking it to explain a concept, check your logic, or suggest next steps — then doing the intellectual work yourself).

Conversation logs quickly reveal which is happening. Design your assignments and AI environments to make scaffolding the only possible use case — configure the AI to refuse direct answer delivery and redirect to guiding questions.
