About the Book

Empowered by AI

A practical, action-oriented guide to integrating AI into everyday teaching — without losing the human heart of education.

📘

Empowered by AI: 10 Practical Approaches for Today's Educators

By Maggie Mosher, Lisa Dieker, & Megan Turpin  |  AAI Publishing

This book delivers ten concrete, classroom-tested approaches for using AI to amplify what educators already do best — building relationships, sparking curiosity, and differentiating for every learner. It addresses equity, ethics, and privacy alongside practical workflows, making it the definitive professional learning text for K–12 and higher education.

Discussion Guide

Questions by Stakeholder Group

How to Use This Guide

These questions are designed to push each stakeholder group beyond surface-level reactions to AI, focusing instead on the book's central premise: using AI to amplify the human elements of education, ensure equity, and reclaim time. Use them in PLCs, leadership retreats, board meetings, parent nights, or community sessions.

🍎

For Teachers

The primary audience for the 10 approaches — practical application, pedagogy, and workflow

  • Reclaiming the Human Touch The authors argue that AI should amplify what teachers do best — build relationships and spark curiosity. Which of the 10 approaches in the book do you believe will save you the most time, and exactly how will you reinvest that time into your students?
  • The Line of Pedagogy When using AI as a "collaborative partner" for lesson planning and feedback, where is the boundary between leveraging AI for efficiency and accidentally outsourcing your own pedagogical judgment?
  • Accessibility in Action The book highlights AI's role in accessibility. How can we immediately apply these tools to differentiate instruction for our most struggling learners without increasing our daily prep time?
🏫

For Administrators

Leaders focused on culture, policy, and scaling success safely

  • Fostering Innovation How can our leadership team create a school culture where teachers feel safe experimenting with the book's practical approaches without fear of penalty if an AI tool generates a hallucination or fails?
  • Operationalizing Ethics The authors state that "ethical, equity, and privacy considerations must guide any use of AI." What specific, immediate updates do we need to make to our data privacy policies to ensure these 10 approaches are safe to implement?
  • Measuring Impact If the promise of this book is giving teachers back "time, creativity, and confidence," what metrics will we use to evaluate whether our AI adoption is actually reducing teacher burnout?
👨‍👩‍👧

For Parents

Questions centering screen time, data privacy, and human connection

  • Transparency and Trust Knowing that educators are using AI to help plan lessons and provide feedback, what level of transparency do you expect from the school regarding how and when AI is used in your child's classroom?
  • Personalized Support The book emphasizes using AI to increase accessibility and gamification. How could you envision partnering with teachers to use similar AI approaches at home to help your child overcome specific learning hurdles?
  • Protecting the Core If AI is handling more of the administrative and grading tasks, how can parents and teachers collaborate to ensure the core of your child's educational experience remains deeply human and relationship-driven?
🤝

For Support Services

SpEd teachers, counselors, and ELL support staff managing heavy documentation

  • Scaling Differentiation How can we leverage the AI strategies discussed in the book to rapidly adapt grade-level texts for students with IEPs or language barriers, ensuring rigor isn't lost in the simplification?
  • Combating Bias When using AI to generate customized materials or behavioral interventions, what internal guardrails must our department set to ensure the AI doesn't introduce biases that negatively impact marginalized students?
  • Taming the Paperwork How can support staff specifically utilize AI as a collaborative partner to streamline compliance paperwork, freeing up more hours for direct, face-to-face student interventions?
🏘️

For Community Members

School board members, local businesses, and taxpayers focused on ROI and workforce readiness

  • Shifting Competencies If our educators are successfully modeling how to use AI as a collaborative partner, how does this shift the skills and mindsets you expect our high school graduates to bring into the local workforce?
  • Resource Allocation The book aims to give teachers back their time and creativity to lead in an "AI-enhanced world." How can the community advocate for and fund the specific professional development required to make this vision a reality?
  • Ethical Oversight What role should community stakeholders and local technology professionals play in helping the district navigate the rapid evolution of AI privacy and equity challenges outlined in the text?
